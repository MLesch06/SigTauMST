About Sigma Tau Gamma

General Information

  • Founded: June 28, 1920, at the University of Central Missouri
  • Motto: A Path of Principles
  • Colors: Azure Blue and White
  • Flower: White Rose
  • Mascot: Knight
  • Symbol: Chain of Honor
  • Affiliation: North American Interfraternity Conference (NIC)
  • Chapters: 76 active chapters
  • Undergraduate Members: 3,200+
  • Lifetime Members: 70,000+
  • Headquarters: Carmel, Indiana

Vision & Purpose

Vision: Building Noble Generations of Men

Purpose: To be a Fraternity of Courageous and Noble Gentlemen who Always Endeavor Forward

The Six Principles

  • Learning: Pursuit of wisdom and creating an environment of education.
  • Integrity: Honoring truth and personal character in every action.
  • Excellence: Setting high standards and living by them daily.
  • Leadership: Inspiring others through responsible and ethical actions.
  • Citizenship: Contributing to the welfare of community, country, and Fraternity.
  • Brotherhood: Forming lasting relationships rooted in shared values.

Creed

"I believe that the true spirit of Fraternalism is a personal devotion to one's ideals... It is not the attitude toward a few selected ends, but rather the abiding spirit in which all things are done."
— Edward H. McCune, Founder and Past President

Philanthropy

Since 2011, Sigma Tau Gamma has partnered with the Special Olympics as its official national philanthropy. Chapters serve by raising funds or volunteering for Special Olympics events, in addition to supporting local causes and participating in university and community-based philanthropies.

Membership Education: Path of Principles

The Path of Principles program supports the personal growth and leadership journey of every member throughout their undergraduate years.

  • Sigma Phase: New member orientation, values, and connection building.
  • Tau Phase: Leadership development and application of Fraternity principles.
  • Gamma Phase: Transition to alumni status and preparation for lifelong involvement.

Strategic Plan 2025

Guided by a long-term strategy, the Fraternity aims to achieve sustainable growth and impact in seven key areas:

  • Growth and Expansion
  • Alumni and Parent Engagement
  • Membership Development
  • Member Safety & Wellness
  • Chapter and National Operations
  • Foundation Support
  • WPN Housing and Chapter Facilities

Leadership Programs

  • Noble Man Institute: Focused on first-year member leadership and values.
  • Earl A. Webb Academy: Annual training conference for chapter leaders.
  • Endeavor Conference: Biannual leadership and networking event.
  • Grand Conclave: Fraternity's national legislative and celebratory gathering.

Sigma Tau Gamma Foundation

Founded in 1966, the Foundation is a public charity committed to advancing the educational and philanthropic initiatives of the Fraternity. It provides scholarships, grants, and funding for leadership programming.

Symbols and Traditions

  • Colors: Azure Blue and White
  • Flower: White Rose
  • Mascot: Knight
  • Symbol: Chain of Honor
  • Coat of Arms: Knight’s helmet and chain across a blue field
  • Membership Badge: Chevron and lamp emblem
  • Associate Pin: Worn by associate members during their new member education
  • Founders Day: Celebrated June 28 each year
  • Fellowship Day: Celebrated February 26 each year
